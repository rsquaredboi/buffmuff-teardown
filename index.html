Why this structure works

  • Each layer sells a different dimension, so they never cannibalize: course = the fix · bump = the acute symptom (bladder) · membership = keeping results + community · supplement = speed ("get rid of leaks FASTER").
  • Non-invalidating frames throughout: every upsell opens with "what you just bought is perfect for you" before expanding the problem (pelvic floor → whole-body aging, bones, hormones, heart).
  • Video-gated reveals: every post-purchase page hides the offer/next-step below a mandatory video ("your next steps will appear at the end of this video") — forced attention before the pitch.
  • Access withholding: member's-area login is framed as arriving only after the "final step" page — maximizing upsell exposure before dopamine payoff.

Steal This — Application Notes

  • Backend-first economics: the front-end course is a break-even acquisition offer; membership + supplement carry the P&L. For a hardware pelvic trainer: device = entry offer → app/personalized-plan membership → companion supplement (validated here with zero hardware).
  • "Upgrade" framing beats "offer" framing: "You Have Been Upgraded" + "Accept My Upgrade" converts a sales decision into a confirmation decision.
  • Personalization as the bonus: the free "Personalized 8-week Plan based on YOUR symptoms" is quiz output dressed as a gift — near-zero marginal cost, high perceived value.
  • Dimension-split upsells: outcome → duration → velocity. Design each ascension step to sell a different axis so nothing feels like a re-pitch.
  • Speed is the cleanest supplement angle: "works WITH the exercises you just bought" sidesteps the standalone-efficacy fight entirely.
  • Trial language on annual plans: "30-day trial + 1-click cancel" lets them charge $147 upfront while feeling refundable.
  • Bonus naming: every bonus is named after the outcome ("Leak-Free Starter Bundle", "Bladder Boss"), never the format ("PDF guide").
